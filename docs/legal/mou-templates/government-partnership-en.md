# Memorandum of Understanding — Government / Public-Sector Partnership

> **Status:** Template / starting point. Not legal advice. Counsel and the
> partner's legal department must review and adapt before execution.

## 1. Parties

This Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") is entered into between:

- **SilkLens** ("**Provider**"), the operator of the SilkLens cultural-heritage
  platform; and
- **[Ministry / Agency Name]** ("**Government Partner**"), a public body of
  **[Country]**, acting through its **[Department / Directorate]**, official
  address **[Address]**.

The agreement may be classified as `national_platform`, `grant`,
`data_sharing`, or `digital_id_integration` in `b2g_partnerships.agreement_kind`.

## 2. Scope of Licence

Provider grants Government Partner a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive
licence to:

- Designate **[Tenant Slug]** as the official heritage portal of
  **[Country / Region]**, with co-branding controlled by Government Partner;
- Ingest authoritative heritage data, geographic admin boundaries, and
  national-historical periods into the platform under Government Partner's
  controlled-vocabulary slugs;
- Re-publish all Government Partner contributions under an open licence
  (recommended **CC-BY 4.0** unless statute requires otherwise);
- Access the Enterprise API for analytics and inter-agency reporting.

Provider retains ownership of the platform IP. Government Partner retains
ownership of all data it contributes.

## 3. Revenue Share / Funding

This MOU is **non-commercial** unless otherwise specified. Where Government
Partner allocates a grant, the grant flows as a milestone-based payment
schedule attached as **Annex A**. Where commercial revenue arises on the
Government Partner tenant (e.g., end-user premium subscriptions), the parties
agree to a **[X]%** revenue-share recorded in `tenant_revenue_share`, with
**[Y]%** earmarked for cultural-heritage preservation.

## 4. Data Residency

All data classified as `pii` or originating from citizens of
**[Country]** shall be stored within the **[Country]** partition of the
platform. Provider shall not export such data outside national borders
without Government Partner's prior written consent and, where applicable,
the consent of the data subject.

## 5. GDPR + UZ + Local Compliance

Both parties shall comply with the personal-data legislation of
**[Country]**, the EU GDPR (Regulation 2016/679) where applicable, and the
Uzbek Personal Data Law (No. ZRU-547) where applicable. Provider warrants
that its audit log is hash-chained and queryable by Government Partner's
data-protection officer.

## 6. Term and Termination

This MOU enters into force on the date of last signature and remains in
force for **five (5) years**, automatically renewing for successive
five-year terms unless either party gives **one hundred and eighty (180)**
days' written notice. Government Partner may terminate immediately on a
declared change of national policy; in that event, Provider shall provide a
data export within thirty (30) days at no charge.
